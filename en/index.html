FOMO Newbie Guide
Understand model launches & participation from scratch

FOMO (FLock Open Model Offering) is FLock's model launch Launchpad. Issuers can start an RMO (Real Model Asset Offering); supporters can participate with $FLOCK during the raise, then enter post-Graduate Model Token revenue and buy-back & burn mechanics. This guide walks through issuance, participation, fees, raise outcomes, and post-launch mechanics in order.

01 — WHAT

What is FOMO?

FLock already covers model training (AI Arena), federated learning collaboration (FL Alliance), and API inference (API Platform). FOMO fills the gap as the model launch and assetization entry: turning models into fundable, launchable RMAs (Real Model Assets) that earn revenue from usage.

One-line definition

A Launchpad for AI models. Issuers run RMO raises and launch models; once models generate real API usage, revenue flows into Model Token mechanics under defined rules.

What problem it solves

Model capability is obtainable, but issuance, fundraising, and revenue sharing are often unclear. FOMO encodes the relationship between API usage, model revenue, and Model Token holders as enforceable on-chain rules.

04 — PATH A · HOW

Path A: how issuers launch a model

For RMA Issuers. The flow runs on the FOMO Launch page; key parameters such as inference pricing tiers and raise bounds are preset by the platform — issuers cannot change them freely.

1

Go to Launch

Open the FOMO Launch page — the starting point for an RMO.

2

Choose target model

Pick the model you want to issue. Each model maps to a preset inference pricing tier and an allowed raise range.

These parameters are fixed by the platform to constrain pricing and raise bounds — they cannot be changed arbitrarily.
3

Confirm auto-filled parameters

After you select a model, values auto-fill from that model. Confirm they look correct — they also cannot be changed freely.

4

(Optional) Bundle an initial token purchase

Issuers may bundle an initial purchase to stay near the Bonding Curve's opening price band. ↓ See curve mechanics

The Bonding Curve stays active for the entire raise; every buy moves the curve — pricing is not locked at launch instant only.
5

Confirm and approve launch fees

Before launching an RMO, the issuer must confirm and approve issuance fees (including the fixed launch fee). See §5 for details.

6

Launch RMO and enter Marketplace

Once launched, the RMO appears on Marketplace and enters the public raise phase.

7

Wait for the raise to end

Raise progress toward min / max targets is tracked live. No extra action is required before the raise ends (success / refund in §7).

06 — PATH B · HOW

Path B: how supporters join a raise

For RMA Supporters. Pick an RMO on Marketplace, participate with $FLOCK, and receive that model's Model Token (MT) via the Bonding Curve.

1

Browse Marketplace

Browse active RMOs on Marketplace — model info, pricing tiers, and raise bounds.

2

Pick a model and commit $FLOCK

Choose a target RMO, commit $FLOCK during the raise, and buy MT at the current Bonding Curve price.

Farther forward on the curve usually means lower MT unit price, but higher launch anti-sniping tax. Weigh tax rate and curve position together. Review curve ↑
3

Track raise progress live

Track this RMO's live progress toward min / max raise targets to gauge Graduate likelihood.

4

Wait for the raise outcome

After the raise: if successful, the model launches and you hold MT; if not, $FLOCK principal is refunded (fees non-refundable — see §7).

09 — FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Click a question to expand; collapsed by default for quick scanning.

Why is the launch fee fixed at 32,768 $FLOCK?

Bottom line: a one-time fixed cost for issuers. Covers deployment, on-chain setup, and launch workflow — same amount for every RMO so you can plan ahead.

What is the anti-sniping tax for?

Bottom line: discourages launch sniping. Combined tax starts at 99%, drops 1% per minute back to base fee, making it harder for bots and snipers to buy cheap at open. ▶ Watch 99% decay animation

Is buying earlier always better?

Bottom line: not necessarily. Farther forward on the Bonding Curve means lower price but higher launch anti-sniping tax; while you wait for tax to fall, the curve may move up. Weigh both — this page is not investment advice. See §05.5.

If the raise fails, what do I lose?

Bottom line: supporters get $FLOCK principal back; trading fees and the issuer's launch fee are not refunded. Drag the §7 slider to compare outcomes.

How do Model Token and $FLOCK relate?

Bottom line: they are different. $FLOCK is FLock's native token for RMO participation and launch fees; Model Token (MT) is the token for one RMA — one model, one MT.
